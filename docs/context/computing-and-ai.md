v# Computing and AI

## Purpose

This document describes the technological developments that made the Solvinter hypothesis possible.

Solvinter did not emerge in isolation. It grew out of several independent technological shifts that converged during the 2010s and early 2020s.

---

# Computing

## 2011–2013

Bitcoin demonstrated that computation itself could become an economic activity.

Large numbers of computers around the world performed cryptographic calculations to secure a decentralized monetary system.

For the first time, computation itself acquired direct economic value.

---

## 2017–2018

Consumer GPUs became increasingly powerful.

Cryptocurrency enthusiasts began building home mining systems using gaming graphics cards.

Distributed computing capacity expanded rapidly outside traditional data centers.

---

## 2020–2021

Ethereum mining became profitable enough that ordinary consumers could generate significant income from GPU computing.

Millions of graphics processors formed one of the world's largest decentralized computing infrastructures.

---

## 2022

Ethereum transitioned from Proof of Work to Proof of Stake.

A substantial amount of GPU computing capacity became available for new purposes.

The same hardware increasingly found applications in:

- artificial intelligence
- scientific computing
- molecular simulation
- pharmaceutical research
- engineering
- rendering
- digital media

Computation was gradually redirected from securing blockchains toward solving practical problems.

---

# Artificial Intelligence

## 2022

The public release of ChatGPT marked the first time ordinary people could interact directly with advanced language models using a smartphone or personal computer.

Artificial intelligence became accessible rather than hidden inside research laboratories.

---

## 2023

Microsoft and Google rapidly integrated AI into operating systems, productivity software, search engines and developer tools.

AI became part of everyday computing.

---

## 2025–2026

AI evolved into a practical engineering companion.

Developers, researchers, students and entrepreneurs increasingly used AI for:

- software development
- documentation
- planning
- scientific research
- writing
- learning

---

# Frontier AI

Although OpenAI introduced ChatGPT, frontier AI rapidly became an international field.

Major contributors include:

- OpenAI (GPT)
- Anthropic (Claude)
- Google DeepMind (Gemini)
- Meta (Llama)
- xAI (Grok)

China also emerged as a major AI developer through companies including:

- DeepSeek
- Alibaba (Qwen)
- Tencent
- Baidu
- Moonshot AI

Frontier AI became a global technological race rather than the work of a single company.

---

# Solvinter

The original Solvinter vision combined two complementary environments.

In northern Europe the idea was to create beautiful, light-filled spaces that improved wellbeing during the dark winter months.

In Ghana the vision was almost the opposite: naturally cool workspaces powered by abundant solar energy.

The common denominator was never architecture itself.

It was energy.

---

# A Changing Hypothesis

Proof-of-Work consumed enormous amounts of electricity to secure decentralized currencies.

As computing hardware became available for new purposes, the same computational capacity increasingly supported:

- scientific discovery
- medicine
- engineering
- creative work
- artificial intelligence

Today, frontier AI requires infrastructure beyond consumer hardware.

However, the resulting AI systems are accessible to almost anyone with an internet connection.

This fundamentally changes who can participate in knowledge work.

Starlink reduces connectivity barriers.

Ghana provides abundant solar energy.

The remaining challenge is no longer computing power alone.

It is the combination of infrastructure, education and human capability.
