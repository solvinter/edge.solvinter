# Solvinter Edge — Phased Financial Plan

## Core Principle

Build People Before Buildings.

Every successful node should produce the people capable of building the next one.

Buildings are scalable because people become leaders.

## Purpose

Solvinter Edge begins with one room in Cow Lane, Accra.

The first room is not the final vision. It is the first operational proof that a quiet, cooled and connected workspace can create digital income, train people and finance a wider community technology node.

The workstation room is the economic engine.

The node is the mission.

## Phase 1 — Months 0–6: Build

The first six months focus on converting one existing room into a usable workspace.

Most work is done by the founder. Existing tools and hardware reduce cost. External labour is mainly reserved for fixed electrical installation and any work that should be done by qualified local professionals.

### Target

- One renovated room.
- Two basic workstations.
- Stable internet.
- AC cooling.
- Basic sound insulation.
- Documented build process.

## Phase 2 — Months 6–12: Operate

The room begins to operate with two junior trainees.

Revenue is expected to be modest. The purpose is to prove the working model, not maximise profit.

### Interpretation

Phase 2 succeeds if the node can cover operating costs and show that digital work can generate real income from the room.

Profit is secondary.

Learning is primary.

## Phase 3 — Months 12–24: Stabilise

By year two the room should become a stable operation.

The trainees should become more productive and gradually take on more responsibility. One may later become a trainer or future node lead.

### Expected Result

A modest monthly surplus is expected during months 12–24.

The surplus should be reinvested into:

- better equipment
- improved training
- savings reserve
- future GPU/compute capacity
- preparation for a second node

## Phase 4 — Year 3: Reinvest in Compute

Only after the human work model is stable should the project invest more heavily in GPUs or compute.

Compute follows people.

Computers can be purchased anywhere. Skilled, trusted and motivated people must be developed locally.

Possible year three investments:

- RTX 3090 / 24 GB GPUs
- additional storage
- UPS
- improved cooling
- monitoring
- solar planning

GPU rendering, AI compute and storage may become secondary revenue streams, but the primary business remains human digital work and training.

## Long-Term Node Model

The first node should gradually expand into:

- maker space
- AI lab
- evening gaming
- homework support
- small business planning
- budgeting support
- 3D printing
- community computing
- local technical education

The workstation finances the node.

The node develops people.

The people build the next node.

## Human Development Path

A possible path for a first trainee:

1. Trainee
2. Junior digital operator
3. Senior operator
4. Trainer
5. Node mentor
6. Launch lead for a future node
7. Possible profit-sharing or equity participation

The goal is not to create cheap labour.

The goal is to create capable people.

## Funding Logic

The first investment is likely family-funded because the project is still pre-proof.

After the first 6–18 months, the project should have:

- photographs
- build logs
- real costs
- real income data
- trained people
- a working room
- a clearer model

At that stage, further development may be funded through reinvested profit, family capital, partnerships or crowdfunding.

The strongest fundraising argument is not a pitch.

It is a working node.
