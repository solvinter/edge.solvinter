# First Year Retrospective

Status: draft

## The beginning

About a year ago I found myself with an unusual amount of uninterrupted time. With little more than a computer and internet access, I began exploring ideas that had followed me for years.

Solvinter did not begin as an AI company or a startup. It began with a simple question:

> Can sunlight become human capability?

Originally the idea was architectural. In Sweden I imagined a light-filled room where people could escape the darkness of winter, work, meditate or simply recover. At the same time I imagined workspaces in Ghana where abundant solar energy could be transformed into cool, productive environments.

The common denominator was not buildings.

It was energy.

## Hypotheses explored

- Solar-powered cryptocurrency mining
- Rendering workloads
- Distributed compute
- Human-centered AI work

## Experiments

During the first year, Solvinter became a laboratory rather than a company. Most work consisted of small experiments intended to understand which technologies could realistically support future work in Ghana.

### Compute

- Tested CPU mining.
- Tested GPU mining.
- Contributed compute to scientific research, including the search for neutron stars.
- Explored running small local language models (Llama) on consumer hardware.
- Investigated rendering and decentralized compute as alternatives to cryptocurrency mining.

### Infrastructure

- Began building a small solar-powered UPS using photovoltaic panels, MPPT charge controller and batteries to address frequent power outages in Ghana.
- Explored how Starlink could provide resilient internet connectivity. My brother has already deployed Starlink in Ghana, providing practical experience to build upon.
- Tested Raspberry Pi systems, servers and older desktop hardware as low-cost edge infrastructure.

### Software

- Learned Linux, Git and GitHub as everyday development tools.
- Built the first versions of the Solvinter website using Next.js, Markdown and YAML.
- Began using AI as a development companion for programming, documentation and research.

## Lessons

Several ideas evolved during the year.

Mining became less central as its economic potential appeared limited. Attention gradually shifted toward distributed compute, AI-assisted work and, eventually, human-centred digital work.

The central question also changed.

It was no longer:

> How can sunlight become computation?

It became:

> How can sunlight, connectivity and education become sustainable human capability?
